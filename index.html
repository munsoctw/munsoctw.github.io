Harvard World Model United Nations
(Harvard WorldMUN)

The Harvard World Model United Nations (WorldMUN) is globally renowned as a benchmark MUN conference and is often referred to as the "Olympics of Model UN." Every year in March, over two thousand university students from around the world gather at WorldMUN to collectively discuss global issues and seek solutions. To showcase cultural diversity and international prominence, WorldMUN is held in different cities worldwide on an annual basis.

To date, WorldMUN has toured 30 cities worldwide, attracting participation from over 110 countries and nearly 30,000 students. It has been previously hosted in cities like Paris, Panama City, Rome, Seoul, Geneva, Singapore, and Sharm El Sheikh. It was also hosted in Taipei in 2010, garnering support from the public sector and igniting Taiwan's Model UN fervor for the past decade.

WorldMUN 2024 has been officially announced to be hosted in Taipei on July 1st.

The announcement of WorldMUN 2024 Taipei has been made on the conference's official website and official Instagram account.

The Instagram post received over 1000 likes within two days of its release, becoming the most liked and interactive post on WorldMUN's Instagram. Many international teams have actively expressed their willingness to come to Taipei and have requested assistance for visa applications

Early bird registration for the conference is expected to open from mid-July to early August and will remain open until February of the following year. The anticipated number of participants is between 1000 to 2000 individuals (WorldMUN 2010 Taipei had a total of 2300 participants).

The core team members of MST have a combined experience of over 150 delegations, 100 chairing positions, and 50 conference organizing experiences. While MUN organizations both domestically and internationally were slowly recovering from the impact of the pandemic, our society has already successfully held several in-person conferences.

To foster local community connections, we organized a spring training session, providing the top 100 high school MUN club members with an invaluable networking platform.

To validate our pioneering position, we independently hosted Re_MUN, inviting the most experienced MUN delegates to showcase exemplary MUN practices. These achievements have fueled our passion, allowing us to form a delegation and participate in the Harvard WorldMUN Conference in Paris, which ultimately motivated us to pursue this bidding opportunity.
